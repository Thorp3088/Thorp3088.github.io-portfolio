My Grandma's Bunuelo Recipe


About

Colombia or any other Latin American country is not where the buñuelo was born. Though, it is from Spain. The Moors, Muslim Arabs who dominated medieval Spain for eight centuries, are most likely the ones who invented it. A staple of Colombian culture, buñuelos are eagerly awaited during the holiday season. This dish, which is simple and incredibly flavorful, highlights the nation's rich culinary heritage. The dough has a distinct texture and flavor because it is made with cassava flour, wheat, and cheese.


Ingredients For The Dough

  • 2 cups of queso fresco (or a mix of mozzarella and farmer's cheese)
  • 1 cup of cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup of milk (adjust as needed)
  • Oil for frying

Instructions and How to Make

  1. Prepare the Dough
  2. In a large bowl, combine the grated cheese, cornstarch, all-purpose flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the milk and egg together.
  4. Gradually add the milk mixture to the dry ingredients, stirring until you form a soft dough. It should be slightly sticky but manageable.
  5. Heat the oil in a deep pan or fryer over medium heat.
  6. Once the oil is hot (about 350°F or 175°C), take small portions of dough and roll them into balls (about the size of a golf ball).
  7. Carefully drop the dough balls into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.
  8. Fry them until they are golden brown and float to the surface, about 5-7 minutes.
  9. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  10. Serve warm, dusted with powdered sugar if desired. Enjoy them as a snack or dessert!
